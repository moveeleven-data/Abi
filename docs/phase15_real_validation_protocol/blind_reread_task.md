# Blind Reread Task

Last checked: 2026-06-18

## Setup

Each artifact is presented with a neutral label and no source attribution. Readers receive one artifact at a time. Ordering must be randomized or balanced before data collection begins.

The reader may not see the reread questions until after completing the first-read questions.

## First-Read Task

After one read, ask:

1. What did you think the text was doing on first read?
2. Which image, phrase, or sentence held your attention most strongly?
3. What felt unresolved, hidden, or unclear?
4. Summarize the text in one or two sentences.
5. Rate confusion from 1 to 5.
6. Rate overexplicitness from 1 to 5.

## Reread Task

After rereading, ask:

1. Did the opening change in meaning after reread?
2. If yes, describe the change without using source labels or system terms.
3. What hidden consequence became clearer, if any?
4. What did the text make you carry back to the beginning?
5. Summarize the text again in one or two sentences.
6. Rate attention intensity from 1 to 5.
7. Rate confusion from 1 to 5.
8. Rate overexplicitness from 1 to 5.

## Opening-Transfiguration Measurement

Opening transfiguration measures whether the reader's understanding of the opening changes after reread. Score it from 0 to 3:

- 0: no reported change.
- 1: generic change with no textual evidence.
- 2: concrete change tied to a later consequence.
- 3: concrete change that reinterprets the opening and changes the whole artifact's perceived structure.

Scorers must cite the reader's own words when assigning 2 or 3.

## Paraphrase-Loss Measurement

Paraphrase loss measures whether a reader's paraphrase strips away the artifact's intended hidden consequence or only restates surface content. Score it from 0 to 3:

- 0: paraphrase preserves the surface and hidden consequence.
- 1: paraphrase preserves the surface but weakens the hidden consequence.
- 2: paraphrase loses the hidden consequence.
- 3: paraphrase inverts or replaces the artifact's apparent effect.

The goal is not to punish readers. It is to identify whether the artifact's effect survives ordinary summary.
