# Human Reader Protocol

Last checked: 2026-06-18

## Recruitment Criteria

Recruit adult readers who can read English fluently and can complete written first-read and reread tasks without assistance. Readers should not be project contributors, prompt authors, evaluators, or people who have seen the candidate artifacts, source material, or baseline outputs.

Recruitment should target a mix of attentive general readers and experienced literary or critical readers. Do not recruit only insiders who already know the Abi theory.

## Consent And Data Use

Before participation, each reader must receive a plain-language note stating:

- The study evaluates reading responses to unlabeled text artifacts.
- Their written responses may be stored, anonymized, and analyzed for research.
- They may withdraw before submission without penalty.
- They should not include private personal information in responses.
- No claim of clinical, educational, or psychological benefit is made.

Data exports must use reader IDs rather than names. Any public report must use anonymized excerpts or aggregate statistics unless separate written permission is obtained.

## Blindness Rules

Readers must not be told which artifact is Abi, baseline, raw model, or strongest rival. Artifact labels must use neutral names such as `Text A`, `Text B`, and `Text C`.

Moderators must not hint at expected effects, desired interpretation, author identity, or system origin. Analysts should lock exclusion and scoring rules before source labels are unblinded.

## Reader Tasks

Each reader completes:

1. A first-read task immediately after reading the artifact once.
2. A reread task after rereading the same artifact.
3. A comparison task only after all single-artifact traces are complete.

Readers should write in their own words. They should not be asked to produce praise, literary analysis, or a theory-consistent interpretation.

## Exclusion Criteria

Exclude a reader trace if:

- The reader did not consent.
- The reader was unblinded to artifact source.
- The reader reports prior exposure to the artifact.
- The trace is incomplete.
- The reader fails a simple attention check.
- Completion time is too short to plausibly include reading and response.
- The response is copied, automated, or unrelated to the task.
- A technical issue changes artifact text or ordering.

Excluded traces must be retained as excluded records with reasons, not deleted silently.
